      • Sale and Agreement to sell

          • Only movable property
          • Price in money or jointly money and kind but not only kind
          • Transfer of property in goods should take place
          • may be absolute or conditional

          • seller transfers or agrees to transfer property in good
        • Agreement to sell

      • Other similar contracts
        • Hire-Purchase Agreement
          • Hirer gives goods to hiree
          • Hiree supposed to pay installment
          • Transfer of property on payment of last installment
          • can terminate contract anytime
        • Bailment
          • Possession of goods is transferred to bailee for specific purpose other than sale
          • may be gratuitous act on part of bailee
          • must return goods
      • Contract of sale how made
        • offer to buy or sell, acceptance of such offer
        • may be immediate delivery, immediate payment of price but delivery at future date, or immediate delivery of goods and immediate payment
        • Delivery and payment in installments
        • or both at future date
      • Subject matter of contract of sale
        • Goods perishing before making of contract
          • without knowledge of either party
          • agreement is avoided
        • Goods perishing before sale but after agreement to sell
          • without fault of any party
          • before risk passes to buyer
          • agreement is avoided

    • Conditions and warranties
      • Stipulation as to time
        • Essence is not time of payment but delivery must be instant
      • Introduction
        • Condition
          • stipulation essential to main purpose
          • On breach, gives right to repudiate contract
        • Warranty
          • stipulation collateral to main purpose
          • On breach, gives right to claim damages
      • When condition to be treated as warranty
        • Voluntary waiver
          • Waives performance of contract
          • Elect to treat condition as warranty
        • Compulsory waiver
          • Accepts goods or parts there of
          • Condition fulfillment becomes impossible
      • Implied Conditions
        • Condition as to title
          • Assumed that seller has right to sell the goods
          • Seller can't have defective title to goods
        • Sale by description
          • The goods must correspond with the description
        • Sale by sample
          • Goods must correspond with the sample
          • Reasonable opportunity to check
          • Free from any defect which cannot be revealed by ordinary inspection
        • Sale by sample and description
          • Must correspond with both
        • Condition as to quality or fitness
          • Ordinarily this doesn't exist cus Caveat Emptor
          • But if buyer had made known his purpose of use
          • relied on seller's skill and judgement
          • Seller usually sells those goods
          • then this condition exists
        • Condition as to Merchantability
          • Where goods bought by description from seller who sells those type of goods, there is this condition
          • unless buyer had reasonable opportunity to examine
        • Condition as to wholesomeness
          • In provisions and eatables, this must be a thing
      • Implied Warranties
        • Warranty as to undisturbed possession
          • enjoy quiet possession
        • Warranty as to non-existence of encumbrances
          • No mortgages on goods
          • if so, then buyer can pay the mortgages and sue seller
        • Warranty as to quality or fitness by usage of trade
        • Disclosure of dangerous nature of goods
          • Duty of seller to warn the buyer
      • Caveat Emptor
        • Definition
          • Let the buyer beware
          • Seller not bound to disclose unflattering truths about his product
        • When it doesn't apply
          • Buyer made known to seller his purpose
          • relied on skill and judgement of seller
          • seller usually sells goods of that description
        • Exceptions
          • Fitness as to quality or use
            • buyer tells his purpose
            • Duty of seller to supply of goods fit for that purpose
          • Goods purchased under patent or brand name
            • No condition or warranty
          • Goods sold by description
            • Implied condition
          • Goods of Merchantable quality
            • Merchantable means usable
            • If bought by description
          • Sale by sample
            • Correspond with sample
          • Goods by sample and description
            • correspond with both
          • Trade usage
            • In ready made garment, implied condition that clothes will be fit for wearing
          • Seller actively conceals a defect or is guilty of fraud
            • doesn't apply in this case
            • right to avoid contract and apply for damages
    • Transfer of ownership and delivery of goods
      • Intro
        • Importance of time of transfer
          • Risk passes with ownership
          • If goods damaged by 3rd party, only owner can take action
      • Passing of Property
        • Property passes when intended to pass
          • Definition
            • terms of contract and circumstances of the case
            • need to examined to determine
            • when parties intend the property to be passed
          • Stages of goods while passing of property
            • Specific goods in a deliverable state
              • as soon as contract is made
            • Specific goods to be put into a deliverable state
              • Does not pass until such a thing is done and buyer has notice thereof
            • Specific goods in a deliverable state when seller has to do something in order to ascertain price
              • weigh, measure or something
              • it must be done and buyer has to have notice thereof
        • Goods must be ascertained
          • Analysis
            • No property passes until goods are ascertained
          • Sale of unascertained goods by description
            • When seller appropriates goods to the contract with assent of buyer or buyer with assent of seller
            • The property passes
            • Assent can be implied or express , before or after appropriation
        • Goods sent on approval or "on sale or return"
          • Property passes when buyer accepts goods/ buys them
          • When he doesn't refuse them within a reasonable time or fixed tiem
          • When he does something inconsistent with ownership of seller / shows domination over the goods
        • Goods sent on cash only or return
          • Property passes when condition fulfilled (Cash paid)
        • Reservation of right of disposal
          • When the goods are delivered by order of seller or his agent
          • When draws bill on buyer and asks the buyer to pay that bill in order to receive bill of lading
          • This is called conditional appropriation (Goods are yours only if these conditions are fulfilled)
      • Risk prima facie passes with property
        • Risk follows ownership
        • unless delivery was delayed by the fault of one party
        • goods at risk of party in fault
        • Duties of seller or buyer as bailee of goods remain unaffected
      • Transfer of Title
        • Sale by person not owner
          • Buyer acquires no better title to the goods than the seller had
          • Exceptions
            • Sale by a merchantile agent
              • If agent in possession of goods with permission
              • Sale made in ordinary course of business
              • Buyer buys in good faith
              • Buyer gets good title
            • Sale by one of joint owners
              • If one joint owner was in possession with permission of others
              • but he had no right to sell
              • Buyer buys in good faith
              • Buyers gets good title
            • Sale by a person in possession under voidable contract
              • Seller got goods through fraud. That contract is voidable at option of aggrieved party
              • Seller sells these goods to buyer2
              • Buyer2 buys in good faith, he gets good title
            • Sale by one who has already sold the goods but continues in possession thereof
              • Buyer gets good title if obtains the delivery in good faith and without notice of the previous sale
            • Sale by buyer obtaining possession before the property in the goods has vested in him
              • If buyer in possession of goods without property in them
              • If he sells them then the new buyer would have good title in them
              • But not if Hire-Purchase contract
            • Effect of estoppel
              • When true owner stops himself from denying the claim of a false owner that the goods are his
              • and false owner sells those goods without having property in them
              • Buyer gets good title
            • Sale by an unpaid seller
              • Buyer acquires good title
            • Sale under the provisions of other acts
              • Purchase of goods from a finder of goods gets valid title under circumstances
              • Liquidator sells stuff gets good title
      • Performance of contract of sale
        • Duties of buyer and seller
          • to delivery and accept delivery and pay
        • Payment and delivery are concurrent conditions
        • Delivery
          • made by doing anything which parties agree shall be treated as delivery
        • Effect of part delivery
          • Part delivery made without intention of severing from the whole lot shall be considered as full delivery
        • Buyer to apply for delivery
          • Seller at no obligation to deliver unless buyer applies for delivery
        • Place of delivery
          • If not stated in terms of contract
          • Goods sold - place of sale
          • Goods agreed to be sold - place of agreement or manufacturing place
        • Time of delivery
          • reasonable time
        • Goods in possession of third party
          • No delivery unless 3rd party signifies that it holds it on behalf of buyer
        • Time for tender of delivery
          • Demand or tender of delivery ineffectual unless made at a reasonable hour
        • Expenses for delivery
          • borne by seller unless otherwise agreed
        • Delivery of wrong quantity
          • Buyer must pay for what he accepts
        • Instalment deliveries
          • Not bound to accept delivery unless expressly agreed to
        • Delivery to carrier
          • Delivery to carrier for transmission to buyer is prima facie deemed to delivery to buyer
        • Deterioration during transit
          • When delivered at distant place then falls on buyer
        • Buyer's right to examine
          • He has this right if he hasn't previously examined
          • Seller bound, on request, to let buyer examine.
        • Rule related to Acceptance of Delivery of goods
          • Buyer intimates to seller that he accepts
          • Does any act which is inconsistent with the ownership of the seller
          • retains the goods after the lapse of a reasonable time, without intimating rejection
        • Buyer not bound to return rejected goods
          • Buyer can just say "nah" and leave the goods at his door
        • Liability of buyer for neglecting or refusing delivery of goods
          • liable for any loss occasioned by his refusal to delivery, reasonable charge for the care and custody of the goods.
    • Unpaid seller
      • Defintion
        • Whole price not paid or tendered
        • Bill of exchange not honoured
      • Rights of an unpaid seller
        • notwithstanding that the property in the goods may have passed to the buyer, these are the rights
          • A lien (right of possession) on the goods while he is in possession
          • In case of insolvency of buyer, right of stopping goods in transit
          • Right of re-sale
      • Rights of unpaid seller against the goods
        • Seller's lien
          • May have this if
            • goods sold without any stipulation to credit
            • Sold with credit but credit term expired
            • buyer becomes insolvent
          • He may exercise this even if he is bailee or agent of Buyer
          • If part delivery made then he can keep the rest of the goods
          • Termination of lien
            • Delivers goods to carrier without right of disposal. / To agent of buyer
            • Buyer or his agent lawfully obtains possession
            • by waiver thereof
        • Right to stoppage in transit
          • He may resume possession when buyer becomes insolvent
          • Duration of transit
            • When buyer/agent takes delivery from carrier
            • When buyer/agent takes delivery before arrival at destination
            • If after arrival, carrier acknowledges that he holds goods on buyer's behalf then its at an end
            • if goods rejected by buyer and by seller to take them back, transit not at an end
            • carrier wrongfully refuses to deliver, transit is at an end
            • if part delivery made, then remainder can be stopped in transit unless such delivery was made in order to show an agreement to give up whole possession
          • How stoppage in transit is affected
            • by taking actual possession of goods
            • giving notice of his claim to principal of carrier
            • Notice to be effectual, shall be given at such a time and in such circumstances that the principal by the exercise of reasonable diligence, may communicate it to his servant in time
            • When notice is received, carrier may re-deliver it to seller or other destination. Charges to be paid by seller
        • Distinction
          • Lien = Retain possession, Stoppage = regain possession
          • Seller should have possession in case of lien
            • In case of stoppage, goods must be with carrier and not in hands of buyer/seller
          • Right of lien can be exercised when buyer is not insolvent
          • Right of stoppage begins the right of lien ends
        • Effects of sub-sale or pledge by buyer
          • The right of lien or stoppage is not defeated if buyer sells goods to another person unless
            • When the seller has assented to the sale
            • Then a document of title of goods was in hands of the buyer and the buyer transferred it to other person in good faith.
        • Effect of stoppage
          • Contract of sale is not rescinded. The contract remains in force, buyer can ask for delivery and seller for price
        • Right of re-sale
          • Under following conditions
            • Where goods are of perishable nature
            • Where he gives notice to the buyer of his intention to re-sell goods
              • In such case, seller entitled to recover any loss incurred through this resale and keep profits
              • if no notice given then buyer entitled to recover profits and not pay losses
            • When he sells it
              • The new buyer gains good title even if no notice was given to previous buyer
            • Re-sell in case the right is expressly reserved in contract
              • No need to give notice and still entitled to recover losses from subsequent sale
            • When property not passed to buyer
              • seller has right called 'quasi-lien' - a right of withholding delivery
      • Rights of unpaid seller against the buyer
        • Suit for price
          • if expressly stated then can sue irrespective of delivery or even if property hasn't passed to him
          • even if goods haven't been appropriated
        • Suit for damages for non-acceptance
          • Can sue for not accepting delivery and not paying for goods
        • Repudiation of contract before due date
          • Where buyer does this, seller can treat it as rescinded and sue for damages
        • Suit for interest
          • If no specific agreement, then can charge interest by giving notice to the buyer
      • Rights of buyer against seller
        • Damages for non-delivery
          • may sue for non-delivery when seller wrongfully neglects or refuses to deliver the goods to the buyer
        • Suit for specific performance
          • if seller commits breach of contract, seller can appeal to court for specific performance
        • Suit for breach of warranty
          • buyer not entitled to reject the goods but
            • set up against the seller in diminution or extinction of price
            • sue the seller for damages for breach of warranty
        • Repudiation of contract before due date
          • The other party may wait till delivery date or
          • consider it rescinded beforehand and sue for damages for the breach
        • Suit for interest
          • Can claim special damages or interest
      • Auction Sale
        • Rules
          • Where goods are sold in lots
            • Each lot is a different contract
          • Completion of contract of sale
            • On striking hammer
          • Right to bid may be reserved
            • Seller may bid on this on sale
          • Where the sale is not notified by the seller
            • Reservation of right to bid have to notified to the buyers, if not it is considered fraudulent
          • Reserved Price
            • Minimum price must be there
          • Pretended bidding
            • Pretended bidding by the seller results in contract voidable at option of buyer
      • Inclusion of increased or decreased taxes in contract of sale
        • If contract is made and performance is yet to happen
        • Tax revision happens
        • The buyer may enjoy less taxes
        • Or may more taxes
